Our Story

Built Out of Frustration. Designed for Freedom.

One person got tired of paying rent on their own home. Monthly for the thermostat. Subscriptions for cameras. Apps that died when companies got acquired. So they built something different.

The Why

Smart home became a rental agreement in disguise.

When you buy a car, you own it. You pay once, drive it, maintain it, sell it when you're done. But somehow "smart home" became different. Monthly fees for a thermostat you bought. A subscription to see your own security cameras. Apps that evaporated when the company got acquired, taking your automations with them. You didn't own your home — you rented access to it.

The worst part? It didn't have to be this way. The technology is simple. Local hardware can do everything you need. But the business model demanded subscriptions. Data harvesting. Lock-in. Cloud dependency. Because that's where the money is for venture-backed startups.

"You shouldn't need a password and a subscription to turn on your own lights."

That's where EJG-OS came from. What if you could own your smart home the way you own your car? You buy it. You own it. It works. No monthly bill. No company deciding what features you get access to. No data being harvested. Just your house, doing what you told it to do, forever.

We built it in Calgary because that's home — and because Calgary winters force you to build things right. When your furnace integration matters, when keeping your home comfortable in -30°C is real, you don't cut corners. You build something that genuinely works, every single time.

Here's the core idea: a smart home OS that runs entirely on hardware inside your house. Your automations work even when the internet's down. Your camera footage never leaves your walls. Your data is yours alone. Every install includes a dedicated EJG-OS tablet mounted in the home as the control hub — this isn't some optional feature, it's standard. You get a professional installer from our network who comes to your house, gets everything running perfectly, and walks you through how to use it. Then it's yours. Forever.

We built on open protocols — Z-Wave, Zigbee, Wi-Fi — because we wanted your system to work with thousands of different devices. Not a locked ecosystem. Not a proprietary garden. Real choice about what hardware you use.

This isn't a startup pitch. It's the opposite of that. We're not trying to build the next unicorn or get acquired. We're building something that actually works for people who live in homes. EJG-OS solves a real problem: the feeling that you should be able to control your own house without paying rent to a company forever.

How This Actually Happened

Not a typical startup story. Just what actually occurred.

The Real Problem

Tired of paying rent on home tech

Monthly bill for the thermostat. Cloud subscription for the cameras. An app that stopped working when the company got acquired. Realizing: I own this house, but I rent the ability to control it. That's broken.

The Search

Nothing existed that actually solved this

Every existing option either needed a computer science degree to set up, locked you into one brand, or still depended on cloud servers (defeating the purpose). There genuinely wasn't an alternative — a local-first smart home OS you could own outright and have installed by a professional.

The Build

Built it. In Calgary. During winter.

Open protocols. Local processing. Real hardware tablet for control. Professional installation standard from day one. Calgary winters meant no cutting corners — your heating needs to work when it's -30°C. That forces you to build things right.

First Installs

Real homes, real people, real feedback

Started installing in Calgary with families willing to try something different. Each install taught us what actually matters. Not what investors want to hear — what homeowners actually need.

Now

Building an installer network

Growing across Canada with certified professionals who believe in the same thing: giving people genuine ownership of their homes. Not a tech company pretending to care about privacy. Actually building it.
