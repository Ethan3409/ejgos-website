Core Features

EJG-OS brings together lighting, climate, security, cameras, automations, and voice control into one clean, fast, locally-hosted interface. No cloud required.

🏠

Personalized Home Dashboard

Pin your most-used devices — lights, locks, thermostats, cameras — directly to your home screen for instant one-tap control. Greets you by name with live weather and time.

🧩

Room-by-Room Control

Organize every device by room. Navigate into any space and control everything in it — lights, plugs, sensors, climate — with a clean, intuitive room interface.

Smart Scenes

One tap activates a full experience. Good Morning can brighten your lights, set the thermostat, unlock the front door, and start your coffee plug — all at once. Fully customizable.

Automations Engine

Build time-based, motion-triggered, or condition-based automations. Lights on at sunset. Locks engaged when everyone leaves. Thermostat adjusts before you wake up. Runs entirely locally.

View your security cameras live from any screen in your home or on your phone. Motion alerts, recording, and live streaming all tied into the same interface as your devices.

Control your door locks from anywhere in the house or remotely. Set lock schedules, monitor lock/unlock events, and receive alerts when doors are left open or unlocked unexpectedly.

Installer Architecture

Built in three layers.
Each one purpose-built.

EJG-OS uses a three-layer interface architecture — one for portfolio management, one for per-home workspaces, and one for deep device configuration. Installers see all three. Homeowners see a seamless consumer experience.

1

Installer Portfolio Dashboard — Layer 1

The first screen a certified EJG-OS installer sees when they log in. A top-navigation dashboard that acts as their personal library — all client homes they manage, listed with status, health indicators, and quick-access tiles. Think of it as the command center for a professional installer running multiple properties. Clean, fast, and designed to scale from 1 home to 100.

Top Navigation Client Portfolio View Home Health Status Quick Access License Management
2

Per-Home Workspace — Layer 2

Clicking into any home in Layer 1 drops the installer into that home's dedicated workspace. Unique top-navigation style with per-home context — device inventory, automation overrides, room configuration, system health, and client account management. Each home is fully isolated from the installer's other clients. Changes here reflect immediately on the homeowner's interface.

Unique Top Nav Style Per-Home Context Device Inventory Room Config Client Account
3

Deep Configuration Panel — Layer 3

The technical layer. A collapsible left-sidebar navigation for accessing device-level configuration, Z-Wave/Zigbee pairing, automation builder, scene editor, system logs, and Home Assistant integration. Built for expert use — powerful and comprehensive, but never shown to homeowners. Collapsible sidebar keeps the interface clean even when every setting is available.

Collapsible Left Sidebar Device Pairing Automation Builder Scene Editor System Logs HA Integration

FAQ

Questions people actually ask.

Does EJG-OS work if my internet goes down? ▾
Yes. EJG-OS runs entirely on a Raspberry Pi server inside your home. Your lights, locks, thermostats, scenes, and automations all continue working without any internet connection. The only features that require internet are optional — like remote access from your phone when you're away, and the monitoring service.
Do I need to pay a monthly fee? ▾
No. The base EJG-OS platform is a one-time installation cost. There are no mandatory platform fees, no subscription required to use your smart home, and no features locked behind a paywall. The only optional recurring cost is the 24/7 monitoring service ($25–$40/month) — which you can start, pause, or cancel anytime.
What devices are compatible? ▾
EJG-OS supports Z-Wave devices (switches, dimmers, locks, sensors), Zigbee devices (plugs, sensors, bulbs), and Wi-Fi devices (TP-Link Kasa, and others). Because it runs on Home Assistant under the hood, it's compatible with thousands of devices from hundreds of brands. Your installer will recommend specific hardware during the consultation.
What happens if my installer leaves the business? ▾
You own all your hardware outright, and your EJG-OS system runs locally on your own server. If your installer moves on, your smart home keeps working exactly as configured. You can continue using it independently, bring in a new EJG-OS certified installer, or — if you're technically inclined — manage the system yourself. You are never locked out or left helpless.
Is EJG-OS good for renters? ▾
EJG-OS is primarily designed for homeowners. That said, many Z-Wave and Zigbee devices can be installed without permanent modification (especially smart plugs and some light switches). If you're renting, speak to your installer — there may be options that work for your situation. Homeowners with rental properties can also deploy EJG-OS in units they manage.
Is my data private? ▾
Completely. Your EJG-OS data lives on the Raspberry Pi server inside your home. No usage data, device patterns, or automation history is ever sent to us or any third party. The platform is local-first by design — not just as a feature, but as an architectural principle. Nobody is watching how many times you turn your lights on.
How is EJG-OS different from just using Home Assistant directly? ▾
Home Assistant is the engine powering EJG-OS behind the scenes — but it's hidden. Home Assistant is a powerful but complex tool that requires significant technical knowledge to set up and maintain. EJG-OS wraps it in a clean, consumer-grade interface that your family can actually use. A professional installer handles all the configuration, and you interact with a polished app — not raw YAML files and integrations.
